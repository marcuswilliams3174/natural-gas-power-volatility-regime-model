# Natural Gas & Power Volatility Regime Framework

## Overview

This project develops an institutional-style energy market regime framework focused on:

- Natural gas volatility regimes
- Spark spread dynamics
- Cross-commodity stress propagation
- Energy shock clustering
- Regime-dependent correlation structures
- Risk-adjusted return behavior

The framework was designed to simulate how an energy trading or portfolio management desk might analyze systemic stress across North American energy markets.

---

## Core Components

### 1. Natural Gas Volatility Regime Detection
- Rolling volatility estimation
- Z-score regime classification
- Stress vs calm market identification

### 2. Spark Spread Analysis
- Power proxy vs natural gas relationship
- Structural spread instability analysis
- Regime-based spread behavior

### 3. Cross-Commodity Energy Framework
Assets analyzed:
- WTI Crude Oil
- Henry Hub Natural Gas
- Power proxy (XLU)

### 4. Shock Clustering System
- Multi-asset stress event detection
- Simultaneous volatility shock analysis
- Systemic stress propagation

### 5. Correlation Regime Analysis
- Calm vs stress correlation breakdown
- Diversification failure during stress periods
- Energy market contagion effects

### 6. Expected Return by Regime
- Regime-conditioned expected returns
- Risk-adjusted performance analysis
- Volatility-sensitive market behavior

## Key Takeaways

- Energy volatility is regime-dependent rather than constant
- Natural gas acts as a core systemic volatility driver
- Cross-asset correlations strengthen during stress periods
- Diversification benefits deteriorate during energy shocks
- Shock events cluster rather than occurring independently
